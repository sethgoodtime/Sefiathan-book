# **Day 186 — July 6, 2026**

Sefiathan woke with the reading's challenge sitting in his chest like a stone. You will never be able to get what you want if you don't go after it. Simple truth. Obvious instruction. The kind of thing everyone had been telling him his whole life. But the second part landed different. The distance between you and what you want isn't lack of knowledge—it's lack of nerve.

He'd mastered preparation. Had spent twenty-seven years preparing to be adequate, preparing to earn his place, preparing to deserve what he wanted. Building elaborate frameworks for understanding himself, analyzing every interaction, creating careful plans for how to approach people without being too much. All that preparation was just avoidance. Just another way to maintain safe distance. Another performance to keep him from actually moving toward what he wanted.

Move before you're ready. That's what the reading actually said. Not prepare until you're perfect. Not wait until you deserve it. Not build more theories about the right approach. Just move. Take action. Go after what you want even though you're not ready, even though you might fail, even though the nerve required feels impossible.

The excavation site was already active when he arrived, but Sefiathan found himself stopping at the edge instead of joining the work. Looking at the warming stones he'd been visiting every day for three weeks. The ones that had woken under his attention. The ones that proved his magic could do something real and useful. He'd been treating them like a pleasant side effect of his presence here. Something nice that happened while he learned archaeology. But that was just more avoidance. More careful distance. The warming stones weren't incidental—they were the point.

His magic worked through attention. Through caring about things until they remembered what they could be. Through odd intense focus that made objects and people come alive in ways they'd forgotten. But he'd been treating that like something to apologize for. Something to hide behind performances of normalcy. Something to keep controlled and small because wanting things, going after things, believing he could actually affect the world—that required nerve he wasn't sure he had.

Calantha found him standing there. "You're thinking loud again."

"The reading said I've mastered preparation but what I actually need is nerve. To move before I'm ready. To go after what I want instead of just preparing to deserve it."

"What do you want?"

The question landed hard. What did he want? Not what should he want. Not what would be acceptable to want. Not what he'd prepared himself to maybe possibly deserve someday. What did he actually want right now in this moment?

He wanted his magic to matter. Wanted to stop treating it like a shameful secret or pleasant accident. Wanted to use it intentionally, deliberately, without apology. Wanted to wake every warming stone in these ruins and see what happened when a whole complex came alive again. Wanted to stop preparing and just do the thing he'd been dancing around for three weeks.

"I want to wake all the warming stones," Sefiathan said slowly. "Not just the ones I happen to pass. All of them. The whole complex. I want to see what this place becomes when it's fully alive again."

"Then do it."

"I'm not ready. I don't know how to do it intentionally. I've just been sitting near them and—"

"That's preparation talking. That's you building frameworks instead of moving. You know how to wake them. You've been doing it. You just haven't tried to do it on purpose because that would mean admitting you want to."

She was right. He knew exactly how to wake the warming stones. Sit with them. Pay attention. Care about what they were meant to be. Let his odd intense focus rest on them until they remembered their warmth. Simple. Obvious. He'd been doing it accidentally for weeks. The only thing stopping him from doing it intentionally was nerve. The nerve to admit he wanted his magic to be useful. The nerve to take up that much space. The nerve to affect the world deliberately instead of apologetically.

Move before you're ready. Sefiathan walked to the nearest dormant warming stone—one of the pillars they'd excavated last week that hadn't woken yet. He sat down beside it without building theories about the right approach. Without preparing himself to be adequate to the task. Just sat. Put his hand on the cold stone. Let himself want it to be warm.

Nothing happened immediately. His mind tried to leap to analysis—was he doing it wrong? Did he need more preparation? Should he have studied the active stones first to understand the mechanism? But those were just thoughts trying to keep him safe. Trying to maintain distance through framework-building. He pushed them aside and stayed with simple wanting. This stone was meant to be warm. He wanted it warm. He was paying attention to it until it remembered.

The stone stayed cold under his palm. Minutes passed. The sun climbed higher. Scholars worked around him but Sefiathan kept his attention focused. Not performing meditation. Not trying to look mystical or impressive. Just genuinely, intensely wanting this piece of carved rock to remember it could generate heat. Caring about it the way he'd cared about the ugly bird, the wooden carving, every object that had ever come alive under his attention.

Something shifted. Not dramatic. Not magical in flashy ways. Just a slight warming under his palm. Like the stone was considering the possibility. Like his attention was creating space for it to remember what it used to be. Sefiathan stayed present with the warming. Didn't pull away in surprise or rush to analyze what was happening. Just kept wanting it. Kept caring. Kept his odd intense focus steady.

The stone grew warmer. Real heat now. Consistent and growing. Not as strong as the fully active stones but definitely, unmistakably warm. Sefiathan felt something in his chest open—not pride exactly but recognition. His magic could do this. Could wake things deliberately. Could affect the world through intentional attention instead of apologetic accident.

He moved to the next dormant stone. Then the next. Each one required the same simple wanting. The same willingness to care without apology. The same nerve to believe his attention mattered enough to change things. By midday he'd woken six more warming stones and the excavation site felt different. Warmer. More alive. Like the complex was remembering it had been built to shelter people, to offer comfort, to be actively useful.

Petran noticed the change first. "The temperature's different. Are more stones active?"

"Yeah. I'm waking them."

"On purpose?"

"On purpose."

Petran studied him for a moment. "That's what you've been preparing to do this whole time, isn't it? You just needed nerve to actually try."

Sefiathan nodded. That was exactly it. He'd known for weeks his attention could wake the stones. Had been visiting them daily, building relationship with them, understanding how they worked. All useful preparation. But none of it would have mattered if he hadn't finally just moved. If he hadn't admitted what he wanted and gone after it despite not being ready. Despite not having perfect understanding. Despite the fear that his magic might not work when he tried to use it deliberately.

The afternoon continued hot and clear and Sefiathan worked his way through the complex. Not rushing. Not performing impressive magical feat. Just methodically visiting each dormant warming stone, sitting with it, caring about it until it remembered its purpose. Some woke quickly. Others took longer. One stayed stubbornly cold and he let it be—not every stone would respond to him and that was fine. He wasn't trying to control them. Just offering his attention. Inviting them to remember.

By evening nineteen more warming stones were active and the temperature in the entire excavation site had shifted. The scholars gathered around the newly warm pillars with something like wonder. Maren traced the carved spirals on one. "They're beautiful when they're working. I didn't realize how beautiful."

Thessaly found Sefiathan sitting with the last stone he'd woken. "You did this."

"Yeah."

"Deliberately."

"Yeah. The reading said I'd mastered preparation but needed nerve. That the distance between me and what I wanted wasn't lack of knowledge, it was lack of nerve to actually move."

"And you wanted this."

"I wanted my magic to matter. Wanted to stop treating it like accident or apology. Wanted to do something real with it." He looked at the warm stone beside him. "Wanted to see what happens when I go after what I want instead of just preparing to deserve it."

That night the warming stones kept the whole camp comfortable and Sefiathan sat among them feeling the reality of what he'd done. He'd moved before he was ready. Had thrown away the manual of careful preparation and just acted. Had admitted what he wanted and gone after it despite fear, despite inadequacy, despite every voice saying he needed more framework before he deserved to try.

The stones stayed warm. Proof that nerve mattered more than perfect preparation. Proof that his magic worked when he used it intentionally. Proof that wanting things and going after them was exactly what he was supposed to do.
