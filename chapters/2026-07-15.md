# **Day 195 — July 15, 2026**

Sefiathan woke to the reading's brutal instruction sitting in his chest: Stop lying to yourself. Not dramatic. Not mystical. Just direct confrontation with whatever truth he'd been avoiding. He lay in his bedroll watching dawn light filter through the excavation camp and felt the weight of it. What was he lying to himself about?

The warming stones work had become routine over the past few days since he'd finally rested. Wake a few stones each morning. Help with excavation in the afternoon. Sit with scholars in evening. Useful pattern. Productive. He'd convinced himself this was enough. That finding purpose through making ancient ruins comfortable again was the whole point of his journey. That he'd solved the question of what his magic was for.

But that was the lie. The warming stones weren't the destination—they were another stopping point. Another place to prove himself useful before moving on. Another way to avoid the actual question that had been following him since Millhaven: what did he want his life to be? Not what useful thing could he do. Not how could he earn his place. What did he actually want?

He got up and walked to the edge of the excavation site where the desert stretched endless and gold in morning light. Four more weeks left here at most before the heat became unbearable and the scholars packed up for autumn. Then what? He'd been avoiding that question. Been pretending the warming stones work was permanent when it was obviously temporary. Been lying to himself about having found his place when really he'd just found another comfortable pause.

Calantha found him standing there. "You're thinking loud again."

"The reading said stop lying to yourself. I've been telling myself the warming stones are enough. That making ruins comfortable is what my magic is for. But that's just another performance. Another way to avoid deciding what I actually want my life to be."

"So what do you want it to be?"

The question landed like it always did—uncomfortable and necessary. Sefiathan had spent twenty-seven years not asking himself that. Had built elaborate systems for being adequate, being useful, being tolerable enough to justify existing. But he'd never just asked: what do you want? Not what should you want. Not what would be acceptable. What do you actually want for your life?

"I don't know. That's the problem. I know I want my magic to matter. I know I want to stop apologizing for existing. But beyond that..." He trailed off, feeling the emptiness where a clear answer should be. "I've been lying to myself that not knowing is fine. That I can just keep moving from useful task to useful task and that counts as a life. But it doesn't."

"No," Calantha agreed gently. "It doesn't. Useful isn't the same as whole."

Useful isn't the same as whole. That truth cut clean. He'd been building a life around usefulness—wake warming stones, help with excavation, contribute to other people's projects—but usefulness was just another form of earning. Just another way to prove he deserved to take up space. It wasn't actually building something that felt like his. Wasn't actually answering what he wanted his days to look like when he stopped performing worthiness and just lived.

The morning work started around him but Sefiathan stayed at the desert edge. Made himself sit with the uncomfortable truth. He'd been lying to himself about being content here. About the warming stones being enough. About not needing to know what came next. All comfortable lies that let him avoid the vulnerability of admitting he wanted something specific. Something beyond just being useful to whatever project he happened across.

Petran called for him to help with a new excavation section but Sefiathan found himself saying "Not today." Not because he was tired. Not because his body needed rest. Just because today he needed to stop lying to himself and that required not filling every hour with useful tasks. Required sitting with the uncomfortable question of what he actually wanted.

The scholars adjusted without him. The excavation continued. The world didn't collapse because he stepped back from being useful for one day. That was its own small truth—his contribution mattered but it wasn't essential. The project would finish with or without him. The warming stones would stay warm whether he woke more or not. His usefulness wasn't what gave his life meaning. It was just what he'd been using to avoid finding actual meaning.

Thessaly sat with him around midday, bringing water and dried fruit. "You're stopping."

"Yeah. I've been lying to myself that being useful is enough. That contributing to your project counts as having direction. But it doesn't. I'm hiding here."

"From what?"

"From having to decide what I actually want. From having to build something that's mine instead of just helping with other people's things. From the vulnerability of admitting I want specific things for my life instead of just being generically useful wherever I end up."

She was quiet for a long moment, looking out at the excavation site. "You're right. You are hiding. We all knew it but figured you'd realize when you needed to."

That stung but he recognized the truth in it. He'd been obviously hiding. Obviously avoiding the bigger questions by focusing on immediate usefulness. Everyone could see it except him because he'd been lying to himself. Telling himself he'd found his place when really he'd just found another comfortable stop on a journey he hadn't figured out the destination for yet.

The afternoon heat built and Sefiathan stayed in shade, not working, not being useful, just sitting with the question. What do you want your life to be? The panic that rose when he tried to answer felt familiar—the same panic that had sent him running from Millhaven. The terror of wanting something specific and being wrong. The fear of building toward something and failing. The vulnerability of having desires that might not be met.

But not wanting anything specific was its own kind of failure. Was its own kind of death. He could spend his whole life being generically useful, moving from project to project, never building anything that felt like his, never risking the vulnerability of saying "this is what I want my days to look like." That was safe. But it wasn't alive. Wasn't whole. Was just another performance of adequacy instead of actual living.

The sun moved west and cast long shadows across the excavation site. Scholars packed up for evening, heading to cooling tents and washing up for dinner. Sefiathan stayed where he was, finally letting himself feel the truth he'd been avoiding. He didn't want to spend his life being useful to other people's visions. Didn't want to keep moving from temporary project to temporary project. Wanted something that was his. Something he built deliberately. Something that required him to say "this is what matters to me" and risk being wrong.

But what? That question still sat empty and terrifying. He didn't know what he wanted to build. Didn't know what specific vision to work toward. Just knew that pretending not to want anything specific was lying to himself. Was another form of hiding. Was choosing safety over actually living.

Calantha found him as evening settled cool. "You figured it out yet?"

"No. But I stopped lying to myself that it doesn't matter. That being useful is enough. That I can just keep helping with other people's projects forever."

"That's something."

"Is it? I still don't know what I want."

"But you know you want something. That's different than telling yourself you don't." She settled beside him, comfortable in the cooling evening. "Not knowing what you want is scary but honest. Pretending you don't want anything specific is just lying to yourself to feel safe."

She was right. Not knowing felt vulnerable and uncomfortable but it was real. Was honest. Was acknowledging that he had desires and needs and visions for his life even if they weren't clear yet. The lie had been pretending those didn't exist. Pretending being useful was the whole point. Pretending he could avoid the work of figuring out what he actually wanted by just staying generically helpful wherever he landed.

That night Sefiathan sat with the scholars but couldn't quite settle into the usual comfortable evening. Something had shifted. He'd stopped lying to himself and that meant he couldn't just stay here indefinitely. Couldn't keep treating the warming stones as destination when they were obviously another step. Couldn't pretend four more weeks would somehow give him answers when he'd been avoiding the questions.

The warming stones stayed warm under the cooling sky. Proof his magic worked. Proof he could contribute. Proof he was useful. But usefulness wasn't enough anymore. Wasn't what he was actually looking for. He needed to figure out what he wanted to build. What specific vision mattered enough to work toward. What his life could look like when he stopped performing adequacy and started choosing direction.

Stop lying to yourself. Simple instruction. Brutal honesty. He'd been lying about being content here. About the warming stones being enough. About not needing to know what came next. All comfortable lies that kept him safe from the vulnerability of wanting something specific.

But he was done lying. Done hiding. Done pretending useful was the same as whole. He didn't know what he wanted yet but he knew he wanted something. Something that was his. Something he built deliberately instead of stumbled into. Something that required him to be honest about his desires instead of hiding behind generic helpfulness.

The desert night settled clear and endless and Sefiathan sat with the truth. Four weeks left here. Then he'd have to move. Have to choose direction. Have to figure out what he was actually building instead of just being useful to whatever he found. Terrifying prospect. Necessary work. The kind that required stopping the lies and sitting with uncomfortable truth until it became clear enough to act on.
