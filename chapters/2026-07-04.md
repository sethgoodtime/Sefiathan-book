# **Day 184 — July 4, 2026**

Sefiathan spent the morning helping Petran excavate another section of the water channels, but his mind kept circling back to the reading. Plan your future together. The words sat strange in his chest after yesterday's brutal honesty about Mira, about romantic love, about letting finished things stay finished. But the reading wasn't about romance. Wasn't about finding someone to complete him or solve his loneliness. It was about the performance he'd been doing his whole life—showing up dazzling instead of real, keeping spotlight distance instead of risking actual intimacy.

You've been performing in relationships without fully showing up. He set down his brush and sat back on his heels, letting the truth of that land. Not just with Mira. With everyone. His whole life had been a careful performance of being tolerable. Being useful enough to justify taking up space. Being impressive enough to earn the right to exist in other people's presence. Never just showing up as himself—odd and intense and sideways—because that felt too vulnerable. Safer to dazzle than to be seen.

The spotlight feels safer than intimacy. That landed like a punch. He'd built his whole life around staying in controlled light. Performing competence or performing inadequacy, but always performing. Always maintaining distance through the act. Never letting anyone see past the dazzling exterior to whatever messy truth lived underneath. Because if they saw that and still left, that would prove what he'd always feared—that the real him wasn't enough.

But someone was waiting for more. The reading said that directly. Someone sees past your dazzling exterior, waiting for more. Not waiting for him to be impressive. Waiting for him to drop the act. To show up real instead of performed. To risk actual connection instead of maintaining safe distance through constant performance.

Calantha found him sitting there, staring at nothing. She settled beside him without asking permission, the way she'd learned to do over these weeks. "You're thinking loud today."

"The reading said I've been performing instead of showing up real. That someone's waiting for me to drop the act."

"And?"

"And I don't know how to not perform. I've been doing it so long I'm not sure what's real anymore."

She was quiet for a moment, looking out at the excavation site. "You know what I've noticed about you? You perform competence with the scholars. Perform understanding with Thessaly. Perform gratitude with me. But you don't perform with the warming stones."

That stopped him. She was right. When he sat with the warming stones, he didn't try to impress them. Didn't apologize to them. Didn't maintain careful distance or control the light. Just sat there being exactly himself—odd attention and intense care and all the strangeness that made him who he was. The stones didn't need him to dazzle. Just needed him to show up and pay attention. Real attention. Not performed.

"Real connection requires more than a perfect show," Sefiathan said slowly, tasting the truth of it. "I've been giving people performances because I thought that's what they wanted. But performances aren't connection. They're just... controlled distance."

"So what would showing up real look like?"

He didn't know. That was the terrifying part. He'd spent so long performing different versions of himself—adequate son, useful partner, grateful traveler, competent helper—that he wasn't sure what remained when he dropped all the acts. Who was he when he wasn't trying to earn the right to exist? When he wasn't maintaining spotlight distance? When he just showed up messy and odd and intensely himself without apology or performance?

The reading's list felt like instructions. Say it back. Overnight bag. Affection. Things that required showing up real instead of performed. Saying feelings back meant admitting he had feelings. Keeping an overnight bag meant planning to stay instead of maintaining exit routes. Offering affection meant risking that it might not be returned. All of it required dropping the performance. Dropping the controlled light. Just being vulnerable enough to let someone see him.

Don't do loose ends. Don't do shyness. Don't do unrequited love. Those landed different than usual Co-Star advice. Not things to avoid but things to stop performing. Stop performing the loose end who never commits. Stop performing shyness to maintain safe distance. Stop performing unrequited love as excuse to never risk real intimacy. Drop those acts. Show up without them.

Petran called for them to resume work and Sefiathan made himself stay present. Made himself not slip into performance mode. When he brushed sand away from the channel stones, he didn't try to do it impressively. Didn't apologize when he got the technique wrong. Just worked alongside Petran like they were two people doing a task together instead of performer and audience.

"You're different today," Petran observed after an hour.

"How?"

"Less... careful. Like you're not worried about messing up."

Sefiathan sat back, considering that. "I've been performing competence instead of just learning. Trying to look like I know what I'm doing instead of actually doing it."

"Yeah. It was exhausting to watch." Petran said it kindly but directly. "This is better. You're actually here now."

Actually here. Not performing being here. Not dazzling anyone with impressive capability or impressive inadequacy. Just present. Real. Showing up without the spotlight distance. It felt naked in ways he hadn't expected. Vulnerable. Like anyone could see all the odd intensity he usually kept controlled. But Petran wasn't flinching. Wasn't judging. Just working beside him like this version—the real one—was fine.

The afternoon heat drove most of the scholars to shade, but Sefiathan stayed at the excavation site. Not proving anything. Not performing dedication. Just genuinely wanting to keep working. To keep learning. To stay in the real connection of doing actual work instead of retreating to safe solitude where he could perform for himself.

Thessaly brought him water around mid-afternoon. Sat beside him while he drank. "You're planning to stay longer."

Not a question. Sefiathan nodded. "If that's okay. I'm learning things here that I need to learn."

"What kind of things?"

"How to show up real instead of performing. How to let people see me without the dazzling act." He paused, then made himself say it directly. "How to plan a future with people instead of maintaining exit routes."

Thessaly's expression shifted slightly. Something like recognition. "You've been keeping exits open."

"My whole life. Never committing. Never planning to stay. Always ready to leave if people saw through the performance and decided the real me wasn't enough."

"And now?"

"Now I'm trying to drop the act. To show up without the spotlight distance. To risk that someone might actually see me and stay anyway."

She was quiet for a long moment. Then: "Someone already has. Multiple someones. We've all been waiting for you to stop performing and just be here."

The words landed with unexpected weight. They'd been seeing through his act the whole time. Waiting for him to drop it. Not judging him for the performance but hoping he'd trust them enough to show up real. And he'd been so busy maintaining controlled distance that he'd missed people actively wanting more from him. Wanting actual connection instead of his dazzling shows.

That night the scholars gathered around the warming stones and Sefiathan made himself participate differently. Not performing insight. Not offering impressive observations. Just sitting with them. Listening. Responding honestly instead of carefully. When Maren asked what he'd learned today, he didn't perform gratitude or perform understanding. Just said simply: "I learned that people have been waiting for me to stop acting and show up real. And that showing up real is scarier than any performance I've ever done."

Several people nodded understanding. Calantha smiled slightly. Petran said, "Yeah, but it's also way less exhausting."

Sefiathan laughed—real laugh, not performed—and felt something shift. This was what planning a future together meant. Not romantic partnership. Not solving loneliness with another person. Just being real enough with people that staying became possible. That committing became natural. That he could pack an overnight bag because he genuinely wanted to be here instead of maintaining exits in case people decided he wasn't enough.

The warming stones stayed warm and Sefiathan leaned into their heat without performing anything. Just absorbing. Just being. Just trusting that his odd intense presence was exactly what it needed to be without dazzling anyone into accepting it.

Drop the act. Real connection requires more than a perfect show. Simple instruction. Terrifying practice. But maybe—maybe—the people around him had been waiting for exactly this. For him to trust them enough to be seen. To drop the spotlight distance and show up vulnerable and real and odd and all the things he'd spent his whole life trying to make impressive enough to justify.

The desert night settled cool and clear and Sefiathan sat with people who knew him. Really knew him. Not the performance. Not the act. Just him. Odd and intense and exactly enough.
